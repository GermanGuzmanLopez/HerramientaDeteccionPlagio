The human face, being the most distinctive part of the body, plays a critical role in identity recognition. Facial recognition technology (FRT) stands out as one of the remarkable achievements of modern technology. Following the COVID-19 pandemic, there's a notable shift towards contactless FRT, owing to its biometric features and increased popularity globally. Businesses are swiftly adopting AI-based FRT, replacing traditional fingerprint scanners, thereby opening up vast commercial opportunities. Sectors like security, authentication/access control systems, digital healthcare, and photo retrieval now rely heavily on this technology. This article explores the global adoption of FRT, its market trends, its applications across various sectors, and the challenges and concerns associated with it, with a special focus on India and its global implications.
